Key Definitions

Hardware Interrupt

An interrupt triggered by an external physical device (like a keyboard or timer) rather than a software instruction.

PIC (Programmable Interrupt Controller)

A chip (Intel 8259) that manages multiple hardware interrupt signals and prioritizes them for the processor.

IRQ (Interrupt Request)

Physical lines connecting peripheral devices to the PIC.

ACK (Acknowledgment)

A signal sent by the processor to the PIC to confirm it is ready to process the interrupt.

End of Interrupt (EOI)

A signal sent by the ISR to the PIC (port 0x20) to indicate the interrupt handling is complete.
